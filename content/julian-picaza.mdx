## Early Life and Education {#early-life-and-education}

<details>
<summary><strong>Background and formative influences</strong></summary>

Julian was born in Miami, Florida, and raised in a multicultural household where the furniture of everyday life—language, symbol, sound—rarely stayed in one place. He became an autodidact early, collecting intellectual fixations as others might collect hobbies: <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Systems_theory" style={{color: '#2563eb'}}>systems theory</a>, <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Taoism" style={{color: '#2563eb'}}>Taoist paradox</a>, metaphoric grammar, and mid-century science fiction all made their way into the mix.

He studied <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Contemporary_art" style={{color: '#2563eb'}}>contemporary fine arts</a>, <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Filmmaking" style={{color: '#2563eb'}}>film production</a>, and later <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Industrial_design" style={{color: '#2563eb'}}>industrial design</a> and <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Digital_fabrication" style={{color: '#2563eb'}}>digital fabrication</a>, building a deeply interdisciplinary foundation. By the time he entered the world of product design formally, he was already a <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Visual_effects_supervisor" style={{color: '#2563eb'}}>VFX supervisor</a> and active member of two LA-based <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Makerspace" style={{color: '#2563eb'}}>makerspaces</a>.

Still, his real education came through working—running fabrication shops, coordinating VFX pipelines, managing projects in rapidly shifting teams. He describes his learning style as **"Muck About and Find Out."**

</details>

## Core Present-Day Work {#core-present-day-work}

<details>
<summary><strong>Active projects and ongoing initiatives</strong></summary>

**Creative Technology Consulting**  
Julian helps individuals and organizations prototype the future they're trying to describe. His work ranges from <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Low-code_development_platform" style={{color: '#2563eb'}}>low-code infrastructure</a> for tiny teams to high-level product diagnostics for cultural startups.

**Systems Design & Integration**  
Whether designing service architectures, managing data flows, or untangling team silos, Julian is drawn to the connective tissue between parts. His approach emphasizes <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Systems_thinking" style={{color: '#2563eb'}}>systems thinking</a> over isolated solutions.

**Software Product Management**  
He's led platform development projects, particularly in hardware-adjacent spaces like fabrication software, aligning team workflows with strategic deliverables. Focus areas include the intersection of technical capabilities, user needs, and business objectives.

**Automation & Workflow Design**  
An early adopter of tools like <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zapier" style={{color: '#2563eb'}}>Zapier</a>, <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/N8n" style={{color: '#2563eb'}}>n8n</a>, and LLM-integrated agents, Julian builds systems that are modular, efficient, and often surprisingly humane.

**Prototyping & Hybrid Making**  
With a background in art and fabrication, Julian enjoys blending physical and digital materials—laser-cut installations, generative interfaces, experimental tooling.

</details>

## Historical Domains & Contextual Depth {#historical-domains-contextual-depth}

<details>
<summary><strong>Foundation of earlier experiences</strong></summary>

Julian's current capabilities are built on a rich foundation of earlier experiences.

He spent years working in <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Visual_effects" style={{color: '#2563eb'}}>visual effects</a> and <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Film_industry" style={{color: '#2563eb'}}>film production</a>, both on set and in post, before founding and managing <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Makerspace" style={{color: '#2563eb'}}>makerspaces</a>. His work in film and VFX production focused on technical coordination and systems integration within large-scale creative projects.

These chapters grounded his design instincts in both creative rhythm and technical fluency—skills now applied to more abstract <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Systems_thinking" style={{color: '#2563eb'}}>systems thinking</a> and product strategy.

Key areas included workflow optimization, technical problem-solving, and the integration of emerging technologies within traditional production environments.

</details>

## Under the Surface: Explorations & Ongoing Curiosities {#under-the-surface-explorations-ongoing-curiosities}

<details>
<summary><strong>Theoretical frameworks and methodological approaches</strong></summary>

Julian maintains a quietly sprawling archive of conversations, speculative frameworks, and language tools—from GPT-assisted system maps to poetic glossaries.

His artistic practice continues behind the scenes, subtly shaping the way he frames technical work. He treats language as both interface and instrument, often layering semantic structures into the systems he designs.

Themes include <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Semantics" style={{color: '#2563eb'}}>semantics</a>, <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Knowledge_graph" style={{color: '#2563eb'}}>knowledge graph design</a>, and the evolving dialogue between <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metamodernism" style={{color: '#2563eb'}}>metamodernism</a> and real-world tooling.

Key frameworks include:
- **<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Convergence_(disambiguation)" style={{color: '#2563eb'}}>Convergence theory</a>** - Integration of disparate domains and methodologies
- **Workflow integration** - Connecting tools, processes, and human factors
- **Distributed collaboration** - Coordination across time zones and cultural contexts
- **Adaptive systems** - Flexible approaches that respond to changing conditions

</details>
