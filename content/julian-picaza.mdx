## Early life and education

<details>
  <summary className="font-bold cursor-pointer hover:text-blue-600 dark:hover:text-blue-400 mb-4">
    Background and formative influences
  </summary>
  <div className="mt-4">
    <p>
      Julian was born in <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Miami" className="text-blue-600 dark:text-blue-400 hover:underline">Miami, Florida</a>, and raised in a multicultural household where the furniture of everyday life—language, symbol, sound—rarely stayed in one place. He became an autodidact early, collecting intellectual fixations as others might collect hobbies: <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Systems_theory" className="text-blue-600 dark:text-blue-400 hover:underline">systems theory</a>, Taoist paradox, metaphoric grammar, and <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Science_fiction" className="text-blue-600 dark:text-blue-400 hover:underline">mid-century science fiction</a> all made their way into the mix.
    </p>
    
    <p>
      He studied <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Contemporary_art" className="text-blue-600 dark:text-blue-400 hover:underline">contemporary fine arts</a>, <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Filmmaking" className="text-blue-600 dark:text-blue-400 hover:underline">film production</a>, and later <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Industrial_design" className="text-blue-600 dark:text-blue-400 hover:underline">industrial design</a> and <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Digital_fabrication" className="text-blue-600 dark:text-blue-400 hover:underline">digital fabrication</a>, building a deeply interdisciplinary foundation. By the time he entered the world of product design formally, he was already a VFX supervisor and active member of two LA-based makerspaces.
    </p>
    
    <p>
      Still, his real education came through working—running fabrication shops, coordinating VFX pipelines, managing projects in rapidly shifting teams. He describes his learning style as <strong>"Muck About and Find Out."</strong>
    </p>
  </div>
</details>

## Core present-day work

<details>
  <summary className="font-bold cursor-pointer hover:text-blue-600 dark:hover:text-blue-400 mb-4">
    Creative technology consulting and systems integration
  </summary>
  <div className="mt-4">
    <p>
      <strong>Creative Technology Consulting</strong> - Julian helps individuals and organizations prototype the future they're trying to describe. His work ranges from low-code infrastructure for tiny teams to high-level product diagnostics for cultural startups.
    </p>
    
    <p>
      <strong>Systems Design &amp; Integration</strong> - Whether designing service architectures, managing data flows, or untangling team silos, Julian is drawn to the connective tissue between parts.
    </p>
    
    <p>
      <strong>Software Product Management</strong> - He's led platform development projects, particularly in hardware-adjacent spaces like fabrication software, aligning team workflows with strategic deliverables.
    </p>
    
    <p>
      <strong>Automation &amp; Workflow Design</strong> - An early adopter of tools like <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zapier" className="text-blue-600 dark:text-blue-400 hover:underline">Zapier</a>, <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/N8n" className="text-blue-600 dark:text-blue-400 hover:underline">n8n</a>, and LLM-integrated agents, Julian builds systems that are modular, efficient, and often surprisingly humane.
    </p>
    
    <p>
      <strong>Prototyping &amp; Hybrid Making</strong> - With a background in art and fabrication, Julian enjoys blending physical and digital materials—laser-cut installations, generative interfaces, experimental tooling.
    </p>
  </div>
</details>
